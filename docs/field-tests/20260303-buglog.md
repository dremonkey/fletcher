### BUG-021: WiFi→Cellular Transition Causes 55s Audio Track Publish Timeout + Audio Route Disruption (HIGH)

**First seen:** 16:55:05 UTC
**Frequency:** Every WiFi→cellular transition while walking
**Impact:** Bluetooth audio drops to speaker for ~55 seconds. User must toggle Bluetooth to recover audio routing.

**What happened:**

Walking away from WiFi range, the phone switched to cellular. Instead of an ICE migration, the Flutter LiveKit SDK created a new session (DUPLICATE_IDENTITY), killing the old one:

```
16:55:05 removing duplicate participant  user-1772517644087  reason: DUPLICATE_IDENTITY
16:55:05 WARN  dtls timeout on all 4 data channels (old session)
16:55:05 starting RTC session  network: "cellular"
16:55:07 participant active  connectTime: 1.9s  (vs 503ms on WiFi)
16:55:38 ERROR  supervisor error: "publish time out"  trackID: TR_AMLh7DykUngWDV
16:56:00 mediaTrack published  user microphone  (55 SECONDS after session start)
```

The 55-second audio track publish timeout likely disrupted OS-level audio routing — when the audio session was torn down and re-established, Android switched from BT SCO to speaker. The user had to manually toggle Bluetooth to restore the correct audio route.

A second DUPLICATE_IDENTITY churn happened at 16:58:43 with another DTLS timeout cascade, plus a CLIENT_REQUEST_LEAVE firing simultaneously.

**Root cause:** Tailscale WireGuard tunnel re-establishment on cellular takes 30-55s. SDK creates new participant (DUPLICATE_IDENTITY), signaling reconnects in 1.9s, but DTLS handshake for media transport fails and retries until tunnel is ready. Confirmed by comparing transition #1 (55s, tunnel cold) vs #2 (3s, tunnel warm).

**Related:** BUG-015 (WiFi→5G session kill), BUG-017 (BT audio route changes)
**Task:** [`tasks/09-connectivity/011-network-transition-audio-track-timeout.md`](../../tasks/09-connectivity/011-network-transition-audio-track-timeout.md)

## New Issues Identified

### BUG-021: WiFi→Cellular Audio Track Publish Timeout (55s)

**Severity:** HIGH
**Status:** RCA COMPLETE
**Description:** Network transition causes DUPLICATE_IDENTITY session recreation. New session's audio track takes 55 seconds to publish (supervisor timeout), during which BT audio routing is disrupted.
**Root cause:** Tailscale WireGuard tunnel re-establishment on cellular takes 30-55s. First DTLS handshake times out, SDK retries with new track.
**Task:** [`tasks/09-connectivity/011-network-transition-audio-track-timeout.md`](../../tasks/09-connectivity/011-network-transition-audio-track-timeout.md)

### BUG-022: Android Background Microphone Silencing

**Severity:** HIGH
**Status:** RCA COMPLETE
**Description:** When Fletcher goes to background (screen lock, accidental swipe, task switch), Android 14+ silences the microphone via `AppOps` policy within 5 seconds. The app lacks a foreground service with `FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MICROPHONE` type.
**Root cause:** Missing `FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MICROPHONE` permission and service declaration. Android enforces while-in-use restrictions on `RECORD_AUDIO` — silences mic within 5s of backgrounding.
**Task:** [`tasks/09-connectivity/012-foreground-service-microphone.md`](../../tasks/09-connectivity/012-foreground-service-microphone.md)

## Session Takeaways

1. **BUG-019 is the top priority** — this is the second session killed by "Queue is closed". The Ganglia `run()` method must handle queue closure gracefully on interruption.
2. **BUG-020 compounds BUG-019** — even after the session dies, the zombie agent blocks recovery for the full departure_timeout (120s).
3. **BUG-022 (foreground service)** is critical for any real-world usage — walking with the phone in your pocket will inevitably background the app.
4. **BUG-021 (publish timeout)** may be related to Tailscale tunnel renegotiation during network transitions, exacerbating the audio route disruption.
